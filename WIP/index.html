What Is Oppression

When people think of oppression, they think of very specific examples, either through history or what’s around them now. What we fail to realize is there is a lot more oppression around us than we think. Merriam-Webster defines oppression as unjust or cruel exercise of authority or power. People generally think oppression is tied with genocide or the silencing of people, but the vast majority of oppression is all around us today.

The United States and its ideals were founded in the age of enlightenment. Very much a product of its time, a few safeguards against oppression were guaranteed to us: a government that respects the voice of the minority, a government that derives its power from the consent of the governed, and most importantly a list of inalienable rights. At the very root of the problem, oppression violates our rights in one way or another. It’s extremely important to educate yourself on what your rights are in order to identify and prevent any attempt at oppression against you. It’s also important to note that these rights belong to you no matter what your country’s constitution says.

You might think none of this applies to you if you live in the United States or another developed nation. If, at the end of this essay, you don’t believe there’s any oppression in your life, then an education in how to recognize oppression will prevent it from happening to you. Apathy breeds ignorance, which provides a breeding ground for oppression to thrive in.

Know your rights!

Here's some of the most important ones

Freedom of Speech

Freedom of speech is your right to say what's on your mind without having to worry about legal action being taken against you. This protects your voice on all platforms that you might use. It also protects your ability to practice what you believe in.

The Right to Education

It's important to educate yourself as much as you can, especially when it comes to your rights. Government/private entity censorship, intentional spreading of misinformation, and high cost of education are all a danger to your right to educate yourself.

The Right to a Fair Trial

It is important to have proper representation in your legal system to protect you from improper perversion of the law. A proper separation of power will protect you from corrupt officials who are complicit in oppression.

Human Rights

Human rights is a very broad and encompassing phrase. In short, it is the right that guarantees your sovereignty as an individual. It guarantees your equal treatment and freedom from discrimination. It guarantees your safety against any degree of harm, both physical and mental. It protects many more, but most importantly it guarantees your right to conduct your life the way you want to. You can’t be forced to do what you don’t want to do.

Where you begin to look for oppression in your life depends on your values and your passions. People choose to tackle gun rights, tax reform, police reform, legal reform, racism, human rights violations, workers’ rights, etc. Every one of those topics and so many more are rife with oppression to a certain degree. The point of this essay isn’t to stir support for any specific one, it’s to get a nonpolitical education of oppression out into the world. Too many people blur the line between politics and oppression. Oppression violates your rights, and rights are bipartisan.
